Banjo Kazooie

- Perfect combination of goofy and charming
- Cartoonish sound effects and music create a whimsical atmosphere
- Rewarding gameplay that promotes exploration
- Playful dialogue with memorable rhyming scheme
- Creative and quirky character designs
- Fun and engaging puzzles
- Refreshing and unique end-of-game trivia section
- Well-paced ability progression

Length: 14-16 hours
Difficulty: 6.5/10 fairly easy platforming, collection could be challenging at times

Majora's Mask

- Incredibly innovative time mechanic
- Surprisingly dark elements
- Difficult but satisfying puzzles
- Mask mechanic is fun and well incorporated
- Unique and intriguing characters
- Constantly refreshing boss design
- Environments are full of life
- Beautiful and emotionally moving music
- Amazing progress-save incorporation of the ocarina mechanic
- Packed with content and encourages exploration
- Funny and charming dialogue

Length: 30-35 hours
Difficulty: 7/10 fairly difficult both in puzzles and gameplay

Super Mario 64

- Beautifully imaginative worlds and themes
- Charming enemies and level design
- Complex movement system that leaves room for creativity
- Diverse music that completes the vibe of any level
- Perfectly balances exploration with platforming
- Allows player freedom and provides lots of optional content including post-game content
- Semi-open world level design allows players to find their own path
- Peach's castle contains many secrets and rewards curiousity

Length: 6-7 hours
Difficulty: 6/10 semi-difficult platforming combined with in-depth movement system

Yoshi's Island

- Forces you to think in new ways
- Child-like and unique marker-drawn art style
- Great sound design (has a few sore spots)
- Iconic music
- Incredibly creative enemy designs
- Amazing boss fights
- Great puzzle-platforming focus
- Egg resource mechanic adds more depth
- Charming level themes and details
- Forgiving but creative health mechanic that fits the game perfectly

Length: 7-8 hours
Difficulty: 5/10 a little more challenging than the average mario game

Crazy Taxi

- Overflowing with wacky and chaotic energy
- Incredible 90s rock-inspired soundtrack
- Cab drivers have distinct and eccentric designs with their own stats and playstyle
- Compressed voice-acted dialogue adds personality to the customers
- Perfectly balances both skill and knowledge-based improvement
- Hidden mechanics such as drifting and boosting add mechanical depth
- Bonus points awarded for risky maneuvers and combos increases the skill ceiling
- Secret shortcuts reward exploration as well as map memorization
- Graphical style and map design build an aesthetic that encapsulates the era

Difficulty: 7/10 doing well requires effective use of all mechanics as well as learning the map layout

Score:
Gameplay: 9/10
Look: 9/10
Feel: 8/10
Creativity: 8/10
Overall: 8.5/10

Tony Hawk Pro Skater 4

- A multitude of maps that excellently blend both gameplay and aesthetic appeal
- NPCs provide fun and unique quests that that both introduce mechanics and further the aesthetic of the map
- Combo system favors variety and does a great job of rewarding difficult executions
- Special meter promotes fluid movement and flow
- Features 14 professional skaters with unique stats to match their individual styles
- Unlockable secrets and cosmetics through the shop adds replay value
- In-depth trick system allows player expression
- Amazing sound design that makes tricks feel more satisfying
- Clean falling/failure animations that emphasize the break in flow
- Stat point system allows players to build into their own playstyle

Difficulty: 6.5/10 mechanics are easy to learn but hard to master and are perfectly gradually introduced through the career mode

Score:
Gameplay: 10/10
Look: 9/10
Feel: 9/10
Creativity: 8.5/10
Overall: 9.125/10
